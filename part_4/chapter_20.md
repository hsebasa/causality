## Chapter 20. Identification as Reverse Mathematics

Reverse mathematics *is* identification theory: which axioms (frames) make which theorems (effects) recoverable — "what must be assumed for this to be derivable" and "what must be observed/intervened for this to be identified" are one question. The completeness landscape of Ch. 7, revisited from inside: the proving arena's equational algebra is the complete positive fragment; its first-order theory the incomplete one. The decidability separation (7.3) reads: causation is simple; experimentation is where undecidability enters. The identification arena of Ch. 23 makes this algorithmic: identified $=$ reachable, hedges $=$ Garden-of-Eden.
