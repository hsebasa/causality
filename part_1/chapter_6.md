## Chapter 6. Causality over a Quantale

**6.1 Definition (quantale; $V$-causal space — every object formal).** A *commutative unital quantale* $(V, \leq, \otimes, k)$: a complete lattice; an associative, commutative tensor $\otimes$ with unit $k$, preserving arbitrary joins in each argument. Derived: $\otimes$ monotone; $v \otimes \bot = \bot$ (the empty join — the production stance one level up); the **residual** $v \multimap w := \bigvee\{u : u \otimes v \leq w\}$ — division in the algebra of connections. Equivalently: a monoid in sup-lattices; a complete idempotent semiring. $V$ is the **value object**; the theory is parametric in it. A **$V$-causal space** is $d : X \times X \to V$ with $k \preceq d(x,x)$ (self-connection) and $d(x,y) \otimes d(y,z) \preceq d(x,z)$ (concatenation). Instances (order $=$ "stronger"; metric rows reversed):

| $V$ (carrier) | order $\preceq$ | join | $\otimes$ | $k$ | $\bot$ | $d$ reads as |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\{\bot,\top\}$ | $\bot \preceq \top$ | $\vee$ | $\wedge$ | $\top$ | $\bot$ | reachable? — **preorders** |
| $\mathbb{N}\cup\{\infty\}$ | reversed ($m \preceq n \iff m \geq n$) | $\min$ | $+$ | $0$ | $\infty$ | latency (5.2) |
| $[0,\infty]$ | reversed | $\inf$ | $+$ | $0$ | $\infty$ | duration (Lawvere) |
| $[0,1]$ | usual | $\sup$ | $\cdot$ | $1$ | $0$ | strength |
| $[0,\infty]$ | usual | $\sup$ | $\min$ | $\top$ | $0$ | capacity / bottleneck |
| $\mathcal{P}(\Sigma^{\ast})$ | $\subseteq$ | $\cup$ | concat | $\{\varepsilon\}$ | $\varnothing$ | the set of routes |

Max-plus (critical path; with the dual order, the Lorentzian/proper-time variant — reverse triangle inequality; Lorentzian length spaces [verify]) completes the menagerie. **On $k$:** the causal worth of doing nothing; unique ($v \otimes k = v$); recovered on the diagonal as the zero-step connection; it cannot be $\bot$ ($v = v \otimes \bot = \bot$ collapses $V$) — in tropical, $k$ *is* the number $0$, the order being reversed: "doing nothing cannot be maximal disconnection."

**6.2 Definition + Theorem ($V$-events; $V$-laws; the matrix dictionary).** $V$-events: $\varphi : X \to V$ (tropical events are arrival-time profiles, $[0,1]$-events intensity fields). $V^X$ is a complete lattice pointwise and a $V$-module; its $V$-atoms are $\delta_x$ ($k$ at $x$, $\bot$ elsewhere), and $\varphi = \bigvee_x \varphi(x) \otimes \delta_x$. A **$V$-law** is a $V$-module endomorphism of $V^X$. *$V$-laws correspond bijectively to $V$-matrices via $A(x,y) := f(\delta_x)(y)$ and $f_A(\varphi)(y) = \bigvee_x \varphi(x) \otimes A(x,y)$.* *Proof:* decompose on atoms; equivariance and sup-preservation force the formula. $\square$ The exact $V$-generalization of 2.3: at $V = \mathbf{2}$, matrices are relations.

**6.3 Proposition (the graded battery).** Composition $(A \otimes B)(x,z) = \bigvee_y A(x,y) \otimes B(y,z)$ (best route through an intermediate); $I(x,y) = k$ iff $x = y$; star $A^{\ast} = \bigvee_n A^{\otimes n}$. *(i)* $A^{\ast}(x,y)$ is **graded reachability** — join over paths of edge-grade tensors (tropical: latency; $[0,1]$: strongest attenuation path; capacity: widest bottleneck; routes: *the set of label-sequences* — reachability that remembers how) — and $d := A^{\ast}$ satisfies 6.1's two axioms: Theorem 6.4's free construction, explicit. *(ii)* The future cone of $x$ is the $V$-event $d(x,-)$: a graded region; threshold relations $x \leq_v y :\iff v \preceq d(x,y)$ are transitive exactly at idempotent grades. *(iii)* **Graded abduction by residuals:** $f^{\sharp}(\psi)(x) = \bigwedge_y (A(x,y) \multimap \psi(y))$ — Theorems 2.1–2.2 hold verbatim at every $V$. *(iv)* **Parents do not care about $V$:** the minimum-support theorem (11.2) holds word for word for $V$-valued mechanisms — its proof uses only equality of outputs; only the *answers* change with $V$, never the anatomy. *(v)* The support map $\sigma : V \to \mathbf{2}$ is a quantale morphism when $V$ has no zero-divisors: "every $V$ casts a $\mathbf{2}$-shadow" is a functor. $\square$ The internal causal language lifts along the same dictionary — graded realizations, $V$-valued verdicts: Ch. 9.

**6.4 Theorem (star $=$ reflection) + intensionality + Generated Equivalence.** Spaces are exactly the star-fixed laws ($d = d^{\ast}$) — **Spaces $\subsetneq$ Laws**; the star is the free space on a law (left adjoint to the inclusion), not an isomorphism. The true generalization axis is base change $\mathbf{2} \to V$: a retract ($\iota$ embeds relations as $\{\bot,k\}$-matrices; $\sigma$ retracts), conservative, with grades as the (causal) excess. What the star forgets is causal: $R_1 = \{a{\to}b, b{\to}c, c{\to}c\}$ vs $R_2 = R_1 \cup \{a{\to}c\}$ have equal stars, but blocking $b$ separates them — mediation structure, visible to surgery, invisible to accumulated connection (in the proving arena: a lemma and its inlining, 21.3). **Theorem (Generated Equivalence: law $\cong$ space $+$ generators).** Call $(X, d, E)$ a *generated space*: a $V$-causal space with a declared **generator**, a $V$-matrix $E$ with $E^{\ast} = d$. (i) *Bookkeeping regime:* laws correspond exactly to generated spaces, via $A \mapsto (A^{\ast}, A)$ and $(d, E) \mapsto E$ — the "$+$ generators" carries precisely what the star forgot. (ii) *Canonical regime — when the space alone suffices:* the generator is *definable* from $d$ under atomicity. At $V = \mathbf{2}$ with AC, the covering relation is contained in every generator and generates iff the order is cover-generated — so generators form the interval $[\,\mathrm{covers},\, d\,]$, with the covers as the canonical minimum, and sharp laws $\cong$ unique-cover cover-generated posets (Thm. 4.6a: the Hasse diagram *is* the law). At tropical $\mathbb{N}$, the unit sphere is the canonical generator (5.1). Divisible $V$ forbids a minimal generator (every step properly factors); the full characterization at arbitrary $V$ is open (Ch. 23). $\square$

**6.4a Corollary (the mediation splitting — the generator made intrinsic).** 6.4(i) carries the entire law as the generator; what the *space* cannot itself supply is far smaller and nameable. Off the reflexive diagonal ($d(x,x) = k$, forced), the strict order $d^{\circ}$ splits canonically — both halves read from $d$ alone — into its **skeleton** $\mathrm{cov}(d)$ (the covering relation $=$ transitive reduction, 4.6) and its **redundant set** $\mathrm{Med}(d) := d^{\circ} \setminus \mathrm{cov}(d)$ of transitively-implied links. *Fiber lemma ($V = \mathbf{2}$, AC, discrete):* $\{A : A^{\ast} = d\} = [\mathrm{cov}(d),\, d^{\circ}]$, the star constant throughout — an interval in the lattice of laws (*The Algebra of Laws* §§1–3). *Proof:* every generator contains $\mathrm{cov}(d)$ and sits inside its closure $d^{\circ}$; conversely $\mathrm{cov}(d) \subseteq A \subseteq d^{\circ}$ forces $d = \mathrm{cov}(d)^{\ast} \subseteq A^{\ast} \subseteq d^{\ast} = d$ by monotonicity of the star. $\square$ This interval is the *free* Boolean cube on $\mathrm{Med}(d)$ — each redundant link kept or dropped independently, every choice preserving the star — so 6.4(i)'s bookkeeping sharpens to an honest splitting with the tautology ($E = A$) removed:

$$\mathbf{Laws} \;\cong\; \{(d, S) : d \in \mathbf{Spaces},\ S \subseteq \mathrm{Med}(d)\}, \qquad A \;\mapsto\; (A^{\ast},\ A \setminus \mathrm{cov}(A^{\ast})), \qquad (d, S) \;\mapsto\; \mathrm{cov}(d) \cup S.$$

The "$+$ generator" is exactly a set $S$ of redundant links — *the connections the law asserts as direct rather than mediated* — and one space hides $2^{|\mathrm{Med}(d)|}$ laws. Equivalently the star $(-)^{\ast} : \mathbf{Laws} \to \mathbf{Spaces}$ is a bundle bracketed by two canonical sections: the **reflector** (6.4) takes the fiber-top $d^{\circ}$ (every shortcut present), the **transitive reduction** the fiber-bottom $\mathrm{cov}(d)$ (none), and $S$ places the law between. Reflection is invertible exactly on singleton fibers — $\mathrm{Med}(d) = \varnothing$, the orders with no $3$-chain ("Hasse $=$ closure") — pinning the precise class where the space alone *is* the law. The fiber size is the $\mathbf{2}$-measure of the autonomy underdetermination (11.3): it *counts* the mechanisms one behavior conceals. (The canonical bottom survives at atomic $V$ — tropical's unit sphere, 5.1 — but the fiber there is residual-constrained, not free, and divisible $V$ has no bottom at all; the structure at arbitrary $V$ is the star-reflection problem of Ch. 23.)

**Worked instance (6.4's witness, expanded).** On $X = \{a, b, c\}$ take the witness $R_1 = \{a{\to}b,\, b{\to}c,\, c{\to}c\}$ and $R_2 = R_1 \cup \{a{\to}c\}$. Unfold the star $A^{\ast} = I \vee A \vee A^{\otimes 2} \vee \cdots$: for $R_1$, $R_1^{\otimes 2} = \{a{\to}c,\, b{\to}c,\, c{\to}c\}$ and $R_1^{\otimes n} = R_1^{\otimes 2}$ thereafter, giving strict closure $d^{\circ} = \{a{<}b,\, b{<}c,\, a{<}c\}$; for $R_2$ the link $a{\to}c$ is already direct, $R_2^{\otimes 2}$ adds nothing off-diagonal, and the closure is the *same* $d$ — the star cannot tell the direct $a{\to}c$ from the route $a{\to}b{\to}c$. Split that $d$: $\mathrm{cov}(d) = \{a{\to}b,\, b{\to}c\}$ ($c$ the maximal fixed point, 4.6a), $\mathrm{Med}(d) = \{a{\to}c\}$, fiber $= \{\varnothing,\, \{a{\to}c\}\}$ — exactly $R_1 = (d, \varnothing)$, purely mediated, and $R_2 = (d, \{a{\to}c\})$, the same order with $c$ produced from $a$ both directly and through $b$. Deleting $b$ and recomputing severs $a \rightsquigarrow c$ under $R_1$ but not $R_2$: the single bit $S$ is what surgery reads and the star discards.

**6.5 The continuum.** Two *independent* axes, both measure-free. **Continuous space:** nothing new — $X = \mathbb{R}^n$ is a legitimate state set; pointless description is clause 3.2(c). Space-continuity is the **atomicity-of-$L$** axis. **Continuous time:** the watershed bites — a generator exists iff $V$'s positive part has a least element; divisible $V$ has no atoms, no step, no "$f$." **Flow:** $\{A_t\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}}$, $A_0 = I$, $A_s \otimes A_t = A_{s+t}$ — Chapman–Kolmogorov over $V$. A law is an $\mathbb{N}$-action (free on one generator); a flow is an $\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$-action (divisible, free on nothing): *no quantale crosses the gap*. **Thick steps generate faithfully:** $R(x) = [x, x{+}1)$ has star $=$ the dense order, and flow cycles of period $T$ become law cycles within $\lceil T \rceil$ steps — ordinary AC polices them; graded over $[0,\infty]$ ($A(x,y) = y - x$ on spans $\leq 1$) the star returns exact durations. **Sharp samples cannot:** irrational rotation ($x \mapsto x + 1 \bmod 2\pi$) is arena-isomorphic to translation on $\mathbb{R}$ — both bijections, no periodic points, continuum-many $\mathbb{Z}$-chains — so "sampled from a cyclic flow" is not arena-invariant, hence **not axiomatizable** (the no-go); the rotation's return at time $2\pi$ lives between the ticks. **Roots:** a continuum law has $k$-th roots in the composition monoid ($g^2 = f$, e.g. half-spans); "the next moment exists" $=$ "$f$ has no proper roots" — the law-side dual of ATOM-$V$. **AC$_V$ (skeletality):** mutual $k$-connection implies equality — at Lawvere $V$, the metric separation axiom: acyclicity was its $\mathbf{2}$-shadow. **NO-ZENO** (over declared convergence): limits are approached, never produced.

**6.6 Construction (building continuous time).** Three ways to produce flows. *(C1) From a semiflow:* any family $\varphi_t : X \to X$ with $\varphi_0 = \mathrm{id}$ and $\varphi_s \circ \varphi_t = \varphi_{s+t}$ — e.g. the solution flow of a *declared* vector field, the differential structure of App. A — induces the sharp flow $A_t(x) = \{\varphi_t(x)\}$ and the Lawvere causal space $d(x,y) = \inf\{t : \varphi_t(x) = y\}$. *(C2) Root completion:* a coherent root system $g_0 = f$, $g_{n+1}^{2} = g_n$ defines $A_{k/2^n} := g_n^{\,k}$ (well-defined: $g_{n+1}^{2k} = g_n^{k}$); if the family is monotone in $t$ and left-continuous ($A_t = \bigvee_{d<t} A_d$), then $A_t := \bigvee_{\text{dyadic } d \leq t} A_d$ is a flow on all of $\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$. Boundary conventions matter: the **half-open** thick step $R(x) = [x, x{+}1)$ on $\mathbb{R}$ has roots $[x, x{+}2^{-n})$ and completes exactly to the flow $A_t(x) = [x, x{+}t)$; the closed step fails left-continuity at the seam. *(C3) Stochastic flows:* the affine instance — 16.5. Sampling any flow at $t = 1$ returns a law; the flow is not recoverable from the sample (the no-go above).
