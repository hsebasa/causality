## Chapter 4. Order: Cycles, AC, and Reconstruction

**4.1.** Reachability $x \rightsquigarrow y$ ($\exists n\, f^n(x) = y$) is always a preorder — the atomic face of the induced causal preorder $\sqsubseteq$ (1.11′); the *condensation* (quotient by mutual reachability) is always a poset: causality always induces order, with feedback classes collapsed.

**4.2 Axiom AC; status; price.** **AC:** $\rightsquigarrow$ is antisymmetric — no cycles of length $\geq 2$; fixed points permitted. **AC$_1$ (strict):** no periodic points at all ($f^n(x) \neq x$, all $n \geq 1$). **Proposition.** (a) AC is independent: the 2-cycle models its negation. (b) AC is a *theorem* in three regimes: token unrollings (1.18ii); *graded* laws — any rank $r$ with $r(f(x)) > r(x)$ unless $f(x) = x$ forces antisymmetry (mutual reachability of $x \neq y$ gives $r(x) < r(y) < r(x)$); divisible time (App. A). (c) Price: on finite $X$ with total $f$, orbits repeat (pigeonhole), so the recurrent core is nonempty — AC$_1$ forces $X$ infinite: the *asymmetry–infinity theorem*. $\square$

**4.3 Theorem (power-collapse trichotomy; AC as freeness of time).** Relations among the powers of a law come in exactly three kinds: **halting** ($f^{N+1} = f^N$ — the world settles on fixed points; AC-compatible); **wrapping** ($f^{N+p} = f^N$, $p \geq 2$ — genuine periodicity, what AC forbids: a fully periodic world has $f^p = \mathrm{id}$, the $\mathbb{N}$-action factoring through $\mathbb{Z}/p$ — *time wraps onto a circle*); **scale-freeness** ($f^2 = f$ for thick laws — the dense future operator $R(x) = (x, \infty)$, idempotent yet strictly acyclic; idempotence $\iff$ density of the order). *For sharp total laws, AC holds iff the powers are all distinct or the world halts.* *Proof:* if $f^m = f^n$, $m < n$, every $y = f^m(x)$ satisfies $f^{n-m}(y) = y$, so $y$ is periodic; AC kills periods $\geq 2$, leaving fixed points, so $f^{m+1} = f^m$. $\square$ **AC $=$ the time-action stays free; cycles $=$ time acquires relations.** (The scale-free escape uses no orbit point — thick steps contain their own composites.) The trichotomy is the shape of the world's effective time algebra (*The Algebra of Laws* §10).

**4.4 Theorem (no token self-causation; torsion of time).** Tokens are occurrences $(A, t)$; production maps $(A, t) \to (B, t+n)$, $n \geq 1$; $(A,t)$ caused by itself forces $t + n = t$, so $n = 0$ — contradiction. $\square$ The proof uses only that the time monoid has no torsion ($t + n = t \Rightarrow n = 0$): over circular time $\mathbb{Z}/p$ token self-causation lawfully returns. The full ledger: *backward causation* — inexpressible (the signature has no negative-latency operation); *latency-0 self-causation* — excluded by definition (the unit); *type-level self-causation* — legitimate (1.17) unless AC$_1$ is assumed.

**4.5 Theorem (no cycles of any length; the scheme).** Define a *cycle*: a finite closed walk $x_0 \to \cdots \to x_k = x_0$ through at least two distinct states. *AC $\iff$ no cycles, uniformly in length.* *Proof:* mutual reachability of $x \neq y$ concatenates to a closed walk; conversely a cycle's prefix and suffix give mutual reachability. $\square$ **Finitariness lemma:** cycles are finitary objects — the only self-production facts are $x \in R^n(x)$, finite $n$ (Collapse: limits add nothing; limits are approached, never produced) — so "no finite cycles" *is* "no cycles." **Non-finite-axiomatizability:** AC is an infinite scheme (one condition per length), provably incompressible — a single $(N{+}1)$-cycle satisfies "no cycles $\leq N$" and defeats any finite axiom set (ultraproduct argument, App. C). Each condition checkable, none redundant: the exact logical cost of total cycle-removal.

**4.6 Theorem (order reconstruction) + Construction + visualization.** A poset is the causal order of: *(a)* a **sharp** structure iff every non-maximal element has a *unique* upper cover, the order is generated by its covering relation (no limit relations), and maximal elements are fixed points — and then the covering relation *is* the law ($\mathrm{covers}(x) = \{f(x)\}$ when $f(x) \neq x$; along one orbit acyclicity forces injectivity, so nothing lies strictly between $x$ and $f(x)$), principal up-sets are chains of type $\leq \omega$, merging appears as shared covers: **the Hasse diagram is the functional graph**; *(b)* a **relational** structure iff cover-generated — including every locally finite poset, i.e. exactly the objects of causal set theory (branching futures $=$ nondeterminism: the diamond needs a relational law); *(c)* **no** step-generated structure when dense (no covering pairs) — continuous time, met from the order side. *Proof sketch:* App. C. **Construction:** given a cover-generated poset, take the law $=$ the covering relation; reachability rebuilds $\leq$; unique covers $+$ maximals-fixed sharpens to a function. **Determinism $=$ unique cover; nondeterminism $=$ branching; density $=$ continuous time.** Visualization: under AC $+$ discreteness, Hasse diagrams *are* the pictures of causal worlds; without AC, draw the condensation and decorate classes with their cycles ($\rho$-portraits, 5.3); dense — no Hasse diagram exists, and the geometry of Ch. 5 takes over.
