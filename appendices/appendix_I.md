**I. Graded Realization: The Language over a Quantale.** *App. G built the internal language on one lattice; its verdicts (App. H) are Boolean. Here the same grammar runs over a commutative unital quantale $V$ (6.1): realizations become $V$-events (arrival-time profiles, intensity fields, route-sets ‚Äî 6.2), and the verdict itself becomes $V$-valued ‚Äî "does $A$ cause $B$?" acquires answers like "yes, within depth $7$." Two design facts up front. The connective's meet must become the tensor: G's algebra leaned on frame distributivity, which a general $V$ lacks, while $\otimes$ preserves joins in each slot by the quantale axiom ‚Äî the multiplicative structure is what survives; the additive $\wedge$ remains available exactly on frame faces, and at $V = \mathbf{2}$ the two coincide and App. G is recovered verbatim. And the graded verdict lives in $V$, not in $V^{X}$: the division of labor of App. H ‚Äî slots take realizations, levels take verdicts ‚Äî is unchanged; H.1's lesson does not grade away.*

**I.1 Definition (graded cone; graded connective).** Let $f$ be a $V$-law on $V^{X}$ (6.2). Set $f^{+} := \bigvee_{n \geq 1} f^{n}$ ‚Äî a pointwise join of $V$-laws, hence a $V$-law (joins of join-preserving maps are join-preserving, *The Algebra of Laws* ¬ß1, and the scalar action passes through joins, 6.1). Define
$$\varphi \triangleright \psi \;:=\; f^{+}(\varphi) \otimes \psi, \qquad \varphi \triangleright_{n} \psi \;:=\; f^{n}(\varphi) \otimes \psi \qquad (\otimes \text{ pointwise}),$$
so $\triangleright = \bigvee_{n \geq 1} \triangleright_{n}$ ‚Äî G.1 and G.8 with the tensor in place of the meet. Pointwise reading: $(\varphi \triangleright \psi)(y) = f^{+}(\varphi)(y) \otimes \psi(y)$ ‚Äî the strength with which $\varphi$'s strict future arrives at $y$, combined with $\psi$'s intensity there. At $V = \mathbf{2}$, $\otimes = \wedge$ and this is G.1.

**I.2 Proposition (the algebra survives multiplicatively).** *(a)* $\triangleright$ is monotone in both slots. *(b)* Cause-slot join-preservation: $(\bigvee_i \varphi_i) \triangleright \psi = \bigvee_i (\varphi_i \triangleright \psi)$; cause-slot meets only laxly, as in G.4c. *(c)* Effect-slot join-preservation: $\varphi \triangleright (\bigvee_i \psi_i) = \bigvee_i (\varphi \triangleright \psi_i)$; the effect-slot meet-linearity of G.4d holds on additive faces ($\otimes = \wedge$), not in general. *(d)* Non-associativity with the same exchange symmetry: $\varphi \triangleright (\psi \triangleright \chi) = f^{+}\varphi \otimes f^{+}\psi \otimes \chi = \psi \triangleright (\varphi \triangleright \chi)$ ‚Äî right-nesting is exchange-blind at every $V$, so the verdict layer is needed at every $V$ (H.5's division of labor, uniformly in the grade). *Proof.* (a‚Äìc) $f^{+}$ is join-preserving and monotone; $\otimes$ preserves joins in each slot (6.1) and is monotone; the sub-meet is 1.6c for $f^{+}$. (d) Commutativity and associativity of $\otimes$. $\square$

**I.3 Definition + Theorem (graded language; closure; the $V$-valued verdict).** Terms: $t ::= a \mid t \vee t \mid t \otimes t \mid t \mathbin{‚§≥} t$, the names $a$ denoting declared $V$-events; realization by structural recursion, with $\llbracket s \mathbin{‚§≥} t \rrbracket = f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket) \otimes \llbracket t \rrbracket$. **Closure:** every term denotes a $V$-event ‚Äî each clause is an operation $V^{X} \times V^{X} \to V^{X}$. **Verdict:** at each $‚§≥$-node,
$$\deg(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) \;:=\; \bigwedge_{y \in X} \Big( \llbracket t \rrbracket(y) \multimap f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket)(y) \Big) \;\in\; V, \qquad \text{guard: } \llbracket t \rrbracket \neq \bot,$$
with $\multimap$ the residual of $V$ (6.1). **Theorem.** *$\deg(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) = \bigvee \{ u \in V : u \otimes \llbracket t \rrbracket \leq f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket) \}$ ‚Äî the largest uniform discount under which the whole effect lies within the cause's strict future; in particular $\llbracket t \rrbracket \otimes \deg \leq f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket)$; $\deg$ is monotone in the cause and antitone in the effect; and at $V = \mathbf{2}$ the pair $(\deg = \top, \text{guard})$ is exactly H.2's $\mathrm{holds}$.* *Proof.* $u \leq \bigwedge_y (b_y \multimap a_y) \iff \forall y : u \otimes b_y \leq a_y$ ‚Äî the residuation adjunction, pointwise; the rest is specialization. $\square$ The verdict uses $V$'s residual, not an event-connective: G.7(c)'s refusal ‚Äî no residuation *on events* at the Boolean face ‚Äî stands; here $\multimap$ is native gradation (6.3iii), and $\deg$ lands in $V$, outside $V^{X}$, as H.1 demands.

**I.4 Proposition (twins, graded).** Right-nested twins share realizations and are separated by graded verdicts: crisp events embed as $\{\bot, k\}$-profiles ($\iota$, the retract of 6.4), $\iota$ preserves the connective and the cones on crisp arguments ($\otimes$ and $\wedge$ agree there), and H.5's witness transports ‚Äî same $\llbracket \cdot \rrbracket$, with $\deg = \top$ against $\deg = \bot$. $\square$

**I.5 Example (the tropical face: depth-valued verdicts).** $V = (\mathbb{N} \cup \{\infty\},\ \geq,\ \min,\ +,\ 0)$ ‚Äî 6.1's latency row. For a crisp event $A$ (profile $k$ on $A$, $\bot$ off it), $f^{+}(\chi_A)(y) = \min\{ n \geq 1 : y \in f^{n}(A) \} = \tau(A, y)$: **the graded strict cone of a crisp event is its latency profile** ‚Äî Def. 1.16's $\tau$, recovered as a realization. For crisp $A, B$:
$$\deg(A \mathbin{‚§≥} B) \;=\; \max_{y \in B}\ \tau(A, y)$$
‚Äî the worst-case latency: $\infty$ iff some part of $B$ is never produced (the Boolean failure), $= d < \infty$ iff $A$ causes $B$ *by depth $d$*. In the proving arena (19.1, latency $=$ proof depth): the verdict of "axioms $‚§≥$ theorem" is the depth of its shallowest derivation ‚Äî and for a composite conclusion, the depth of its deepest part. On the strength face $([0,1], \leq, \sup, \cdot, 1)$: $\deg(A \mathbin{‚§≥} B) = \min_{y \in B} f^{+}(\chi_A)(y)$ ‚Äî the strongest-route attenuation of the worst-served part of $B$. *Proof.* The tropical residual is truncated subtraction with $k \multimap v = v$; off-$B$ factors are $k$, the $\preceq$-meet over $B$ is the numerical maximum; the $[0,1]$ case likewise with capped division. $\square$

**I.6 Remark (shadows; what does not lift).** When $V$ has no zero-divisors, the support morphism $\sigma : V \to \mathbf{2}$ (6.3v) sends the graded calculus onto the Boolean one clause by clause ‚Äî $\sigma \llbracket t \rrbracket_{V} = \llbracket t \rrbracket_{\mathbf{2}}$: every graded realization casts G's shadow. Verdicts do *not* shadow in general ($\sigma$ need not preserve meets): the grade carries strictly more than its Boolean collapse, which is the point. And the boundary, once more: the affine-stochastic face is not a quantale ‚Äî no idempotent join, no convergent star (5.2, 2.5iii) ‚Äî so there is **no measure-valued realization**; chance enters by evaluating realizations, once and late (1.22, Ch. 13, App. B). The map of homes is H.11.
