**H. The Causal Extension.** *App. G internalized causation as an operator $\triangleright$ on $L$ itself, definable from $f$; but $\triangleright$-nesting is exchange-symmetric ‚Äî $A \triangleright (B \triangleright C) = f^{+}(A) \wedge f^{+}(B) \wedge C = B \triangleright (A \triangleright C)$ ‚Äî so it cannot distinguish "$A$ causes ($B$ causes $C$)" from "$B$ causes ($A$ causes $C$)": it is not faithful to nested causal claims. To make a causal proposition a first-class **event** that can itself be caused, we enlarge the lattice with an event "$A$ causes $B$" and extend the law to it. We read "causes" as **reachability** ‚Äî the causal preorder $\sqsubseteq$ of 1.11‚Ä≤, $B \le f^{*}(A)$ ‚Äî for two reasons proved below: it is the reading under which the natural extension of the law carries true causal claims to true ones (H.5), and under which "$A$ causes everything $B$ causes" is just transitivity, not a defect. Throughout, $f^{*} = \bigvee_{n} f^{n}$ is the reachability closure (1.14), forward-closed by Lemma 1.11, and we verify that $(L^{exp}, f^{exp})$ is itself a causal structure (Def. 1.5). The law $f^{exp}$ is one-step (it advances by one application of $f$); reachability enters only as the truth criterion for causal claims.*

**H.1 Definition (causal terms; closure under the causal former).** Fix a causal structure $(L, f)$. The set $\mathrm{Cau}(L)$ of **causal terms** is the *least* set with (i) $L \subseteq \mathrm{Cau}(L)$; (ii) $s, t \in \mathrm{Cau}(L) \implies (s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) \in \mathrm{Cau}(L)$. Read $s \mathbin{‚§≥} t$ as "$s$ causes $t$"; $s$ is the **cause**, $t$ the **conclusion**. Clause (ii) is exactly what **closed under the causal former** means: forming "$s$ causes $t$" never leaves the set. Each term has a finite **rank** $\mathrm{rk}(a) = 0$ ($a \in L$), $\mathrm{rk}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) = 1 + \max(\mathrm{rk}\,s, \mathrm{rk}\,t)$, on which the recursions below induct. Carry the **generated preorder**: the least preorder making $L \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Cau}(L)$ order-preserving and the former monotone in both arguments ‚Äî $s \mathbin{‚§≥} t \sqsubseteq s' \mathbin{‚§≥} t'$ whenever $s \sqsubseteq s'$ and $t \sqsubseteq t'$. (Covariance in both matches the realized content $f^{*}(s) \wedge t$, which is monotone in each; only conclusion-monotonicity is used in the proofs.)

**H.2 Definition (the extended lattice $L^{exp}$).** $L^{exp}$ is the join-completion of $(\mathrm{Cau}(L), \sqsubseteq)$ *relative to the joins of $L$*: a complete lattice equipped with
- **(E1)** a join-preserving embedding $\iota : L \hookrightarrow L^{exp}$ (identify $L$ with its image; $\iota$ preserves all existing joins, so $\bot_{L} = \bot$);
- **(E2)** a total operation $\mathbin{‚§≥} : L^{exp} \times L^{exp} \to L^{exp}$, monotone (covariant) in each argument, extending the former of H.1;
- **(E3)** the causal terms are **join-dense**, and the only join-relations among terms are those of $L$: every $x \in L^{exp}$ is $\bigvee\{\tau \in \mathrm{Cau}(L) : \tau \le x\}$, the embedded $L$ retains exactly its own joins, and each causal term $s \mathbin{‚§≥} t \notin L$ is completely join-prime ($s \mathbin{‚§≥} t \le \bigvee_{i} x_{i} \Rightarrow s \mathbin{‚§≥} t \le x_{i}$ for some $i$).

The lattice join interprets "**or**"; $\wedge, \top, \bot$ exist by completeness ("and" is available, not needed below). **Two senses of "closed."** *(a)* The generators $\mathrm{Cau}(L)$ are closed under the former $\mathbin{‚§≥}$ (H.1), and $L$ under its joins. *(b)* $L^{exp}$ is closed under arbitrary joins (it is complete) and under $\mathbin{‚§≥}$ (E2 is total): so "or" and "causes" never leave $L^{exp}$, and $L^{exp}$ contains **every** causal expression over $L$. **Existence.** Present the complete lattice by generators $\mathrm{Cau}(L)$ and relations = (the joins of $L$) $+$ (monotonicity of $\mathbin{‚§≥}$); concretely, the closure system of $\sqsubseteq$-down-sets closed under $L$'s joins, with $\iota(a) = $ the closure of ${\downarrow}a$. Since the former imposes no join-identity on its arguments, no causal term is a join of others ‚Äî giving the join-primality of (E3). **Why not restrict to valid events only.** One might want $L^{exp}$ to contain *only* causal facts that hold. This conflicts with closure: for arbitrary $A, B \in L^{exp}$ the claim $A \mathbin{‚§≥} B$ need not hold, so a lattice both closed under a *total* $\mathbin{‚§≥}$ and containing only holding facts cannot exist. We therefore keep $\mathbin{‚§≥}$ total ‚Äî every expression is an event ‚Äî and mark the holding ones by a predicate (H.5); the holding facts are closed under the law (H.5), and are the genuine causal content.

**H.3 Definition + Lemma (the extended law).** Define $f^{exp}$ on causal terms by recursion on rank:
$$f^{exp}(a) = f(a)\quad (a \in L),\qquad f^{exp}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) = s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t).$$
**Lemma.** *This is well-defined, sends terms to terms, is rank-non-increasing and monotone, and extends uniquely to a join-preserving endomap $f^{exp} : L^{exp} \to L^{exp}$.* *Proof.* Well-foundedness: the clause for $s \mathbin{‚§≥} t$ recurses on the conclusion $t$, of strictly smaller rank, and bottoms out on $L$. Terms to terms: $f(a) \in L \subseteq \mathrm{Cau}(L)$, and $s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t)$ is a term whenever $f^{exp}(t)$ is. Rank-non-increasing: by induction $\mathrm{rk}(f^{exp}(t)) \le \mathrm{rk}(t)$, so $\mathrm{rk}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t)) \le \mathrm{rk}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t)$. Monotone (for the generated preorder): $f$ is monotone, and if $t \sqsubseteq t'$ then $s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t) \sqsubseteq s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t')$ by E2 and induction. Finally, $f^{exp}$ restricted to $L$ is $f$, which preserves $L$'s joins, and no causal term is a join of other terms (E3); hence $f^{exp}$ preserves every join holding among terms and extends uniquely to a join-preserving endomap of $L^{exp}$ (a join-preserving map is determined by its values on the join-dense terms, and these values are join-consistent). $\square$

**H.4 Proposition ($(L^{exp}, f^{exp})$ is a causal structure).** *$L^{exp}$ is a complete lattice and $f^{exp}$ preserves arbitrary joins; hence $(L^{exp}, f^{exp})$ satisfies the one axiom (Def. 1.5). Moreover $f^{exp}$ extends $f$ (conservativity), and $f^{*}_{exp}(a) = f^{*}(a)$ for every $a \in L$.* *Proof.* Completeness is H.2; join-preservation is H.3; then $f^{exp}(\bot) = \bot$ (the $I = \varnothing$ case, 1.6a). Conservativity is the base clause. For $a \in L$: $(f^{exp})^{n}(a) = f^{n}(a) \in L$ by induction ($L$ is $f$-closed), so $f^{*}_{exp}(a) = \bigvee_{n} (f^{exp})^{n}(a) = \bigvee_{n} f^{n}(a) = f^{*}(a)$ (1.14). $\square$ Thus $(L^{exp}, f^{exp})$ carries its own reachability closure $f^{*}_{exp}$ and causal preorder $\sqsubseteq_{exp}$ (1.11‚Ä≤), restricting on $L$ to $f^{*}$ and $\sqsubseteq$.

**H.5 Definition + Theorem (causal claims, and their preservation by the law).** A causal event **holds** ‚Äî is a genuine fact ‚Äî exactly when its cause reaches its conclusion in the extended structure:
$$A \mathbin{‚§≥} B \text{ holds} \quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad A \sqsubseteq_{exp} B \quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad B \le f^{*}_{exp}(A)\qquad(1.11').$$
On base events this is $B \le f^{*}(A)$ (H.4) ‚Äî the original causal preorder, so the reading is conservative. **Theorem (the law preserves truth).** *If $A \mathbin{‚§≥} B$ holds, so does $f^{exp}(A \mathbin{‚§≥} B)$.* *Proof.* $f^{exp}$ is join-preserving (H.3), so by Lemma 1.11 its reachability $f^{*}_{exp}$ is a closure operator whose values are forward-closed: $f^{exp}(f^{*}_{exp}(A)) \le f^{*}_{exp}(A)$. Hence
$$B \le f^{*}_{exp}(A) \;\Longrightarrow\; f^{exp}(B) \le f^{exp}\big(f^{*}_{exp}(A)\big) \le f^{*}_{exp}(A),$$
i.e. $A \sqsubseteq_{exp} f^{exp}(B)$. Since $f^{exp}(A \mathbin{‚§≥} B) = A \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(B)$, the produced event holds. $\square$ **This is the justification of advancing the conclusion:** under the reachability reading the law carries true facts to true facts ‚Äî it "breaks nothing" ‚Äî *precisely because reachability is forward-closed* (1.9, 1.11). The holding facts are therefore **closed under $f^{exp}$** ‚Äî the genuine causal content propagates as the dynamics runs ‚Äî while a non-holding $A \mathbin{‚§≥} B$ is a formal claim that is simply never realized. This is the precise sense in which "$A$ causes $B$" is *well-defined* exactly when it holds, without having to excise the other events from the lattice (H.2). **Remark (inheritance is transitivity).** $\sqsubseteq_{exp}$ is a preorder (1.11‚Ä≤), so from $A \mathbin{‚§≥} B$ the law generates $A \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(B), A \mathbin{‚§≥} (f^{exp})^{2}(B), \dots$ ‚Äî that is, $A \mathbin{‚§≥} E$ for every $E$ in $B$'s forward orbit ‚Äî and each holds because $A \sqsubseteq_{exp} B \sqsubseteq_{exp} E$. The "$A$ causes everything $B$ causes" that looked like a defect for one-step production is, under reachability, exactly transitivity of $\sqsubseteq_{exp}$ ‚Äî hence correct, not spurious.

**H.6 Theorem (existence and uniqueness of $f^{exp}$).** *For maps $g : L^{exp} \to L^{exp}$ consider:* **(Œõ)** *$g$ preserves arbitrary joins;* **(C)** *$g(a) = f(a)$ for $a \in L$;* **(A)** *$g(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) \le s \mathbin{‚§≥} g(t)$ for all $s, t$ (advance the conclusion ‚Äî an inequality, with $g(t)$, since $t$ may be a cause);* **(M)** *$g$ is the greatest map satisfying (Œõ), (C), (A).* *These determine a unique map ‚Äî the $f^{exp}$ of H.3 ‚Äî and it satisfies (A) with equality.* *Proof.* The map of H.3 satisfies (Œõ), (C), and (A) with equality. For uniqueness via maximality, let $g$ satisfy (Œõ), (C), (A); we show $g \le f^{exp}$. *On terms*, by induction on rank: $g(a) = f(a) = f^{exp}(a)$ by (C); and
$$g(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) \;\overset{\text{(A)}}{\le}\; s \mathbin{‚§≥} g(t) \;\overset{\text{IH}}{\le}\; s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t) \;=\; f^{exp}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t),$$
using the induction hypothesis $g(t) \le f^{exp}(t)$ (proper subterm), monotonicity of $\mathbin{‚§≥}$ in the conclusion (E2), and H.3. *On joins:* for $x = \bigvee_{i} \tau_{i}$ with $\tau_{i}$ terms (E3), $g(x) = \bigvee_{i} g(\tau_{i}) \le \bigvee_{i} f^{exp}(\tau_{i}) = f^{exp}(x)$ by (Œõ). Hence $f^{exp}$ is the greatest map satisfying (Œõ), (C), (A); (M) selects it. If $g_{1}, g_{2}$ satisfy all four, each is greatest, so $g_{1} \le g_{2} \le g_{1}$. $\square$

**H.6‚Ä≤ Proposition ((M) is essential).** *(Œõ), (C), (A) alone do not determine $g$.* The map $g_{\bot}$ sending $a \in L$ to $f(a)$ and every causal term to $\bot$ (extended by joins) satisfies (Œõ), (C), and (A) ‚Äî the last because $\bot \le s \mathbin{‚§≥} g_{\bot}(t)$ trivially ‚Äî yet $g_{\bot} \neq f^{exp}$: it is the *least* extension, annihilating every causal fact. (M) discards it, and the greatest solution saturates (A) to the equality $f^{exp}(s \mathbin{‚§≥} t) = s \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{exp}(t)$ of H.3. $\square$

**H.7 Remarks.** *(a) The law is one-step, not $f^{*}$.* Validity is reachability, but the **law** advances by one application of $f$. This is forced: $(L^{exp}, f^{exp})$ is a causal structure only if $f^{exp}$ is a single join-preserving endomap (Def. 1.5), whose iterates *then* define $f^{*}_{exp}$; setting $f^{exp} = f^{*}$ would use a closure as the law, and since $f^{*}$ is idempotent the dynamics would freeze after one step. Reachability is the truth criterion (H.5), not the step. *(b) Action on a nested expression.* $f^{exp}$ advances exactly the conclusion-position base events, holding every cause:
$$f^{exp}\big((A \mathbin{‚§≥} X) \mathbin{‚§≥} (B \mathbin{‚§≥} (C \mathbin{‚§≥} (D \vee (H \mathbin{‚§≥} F))))\big) = (A \mathbin{‚§≥} X) \mathbin{‚§≥} \big(B \mathbin{‚§≥} \big(C \mathbin{‚§≥} \big(f(D) \vee (H \mathbin{‚§≥} f(F))\big)\big)\big).$$
*(c) Determination by $f$, and termination.* By H.3, $f^{exp}$ is entirely determined by $f$. The reduction to $L$ is **structural recursion on rank** (finite, hence terminating) ‚Äî *not* iteration of $f^{exp}$, which advances the conclusion indefinitely ($(f^{exp})^{n}(A \mathbin{‚§≥} B) = A \mathbin{‚§≥} f^{n}(B)$, and $f^{n}(B)$ may cycle, as in the clock 1.7c/1.17) and so need not ever enter $L$. *(d) Why reachability, not one-step.* The truth-preservation of H.5 needs $f^{*}_{exp}$ forward-closed; the one-step relation $B \le f(A)$ is not forward-closed ($f(B) \not\le f(A)$ in general ‚Äî e.g. translation, 1.7b), so an extension built on one-step production would carry the true fact $A \mathbin{‚§≥} B$ to the false $A \mathbin{‚§≥} f(B)$. Reachability ($\sqsubseteq$, 1.11‚Ä≤) is exactly the forward-closed, transitive reading that makes advance-the-conclusion sound. *(e) Faithfulness of nesting.* Unlike the operator $\triangleright$ of App. G (exchange-symmetric), in $L^{exp}$ the events $A \mathbin{‚§≥} (B \mathbin{‚§≥} C)$ and $B \mathbin{‚§≥} (A \mathbin{‚§≥} C)$ are distinct join-prime terms, so nested causal order is retained.
