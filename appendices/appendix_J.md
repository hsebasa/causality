**J. The Regime Arena: Causing Mechanisms.** *App. H closed with a boundary (H.10): "$X$ brings it about that the mechanism $A \to B$ is in place" needs a law that varies with the state, and lies outside the one-fixed-law scope of G–H. This appendix builds the home that H.10 pointed to and* The Algebra of Laws *§8 prepared: an arena whose states carry the law in force. Three results. Law-level facts become genuine events there — 21.1 is relativized, not repealed. H.1 turns from obstruction into boundary theorem: mechanism change is causable exactly across a live transition — a frozen law cannot produce its own reform. And the language of G–H applies verbatim, so nested mechanism claims are exactly evaluable. The cost is one declaration — the transition rule $\rho$ — and the cost is the point (1.22): one cannot ask what changes the law without saying how the law changes. Sharp presentation; relational case analogous (2.3).*

**J.1 Definition (regime arena; cylinders; mechanism events).** Data: a state set $X$; a declared set $\mathcal{R}$ of **regimes** — state maps $g : X \to X$ (2.3); a **transition rule** $\rho : X \times \mathcal{R} \to \mathcal{R}$ — which law is in force next, given what is the case now. The **regime arena** is $\hat{X} := X \times \mathcal{R}$ with
$$\hat{f}(x, g) \;:=\; \big(g(x),\ \rho(x, g)\big), \qquad \hat{L} := \mathcal{P}(\hat{X}):$$
the regime in force takes the world step; the transition rule names its successor. A sharp map's images preserve unions, so $(\hat{L}, \hat{f})$ is a causal structure (1.5, 1.7a) — the fold of the law into the state promised by the regime-change reading (G.7c, 1.18), on a carrier drawn from the lattice of mechanisms (*The Algebra of Laws* §8). **Cylinders:** the world event $\langle A \rangle := A \times \mathcal{R}$ ("the state is in $A$, whatever the law") and the **mechanism event** $[M] := X \times M$ for $M \subseteq \mathcal{R}$ ("a regime in $M$ is in force") — e.g. $[M_{A \to B}]$ with $M_{A \to B} := \{ g \in \mathcal{R} : g[A] \subseteq B \}$, "the mechanism $A \to B$ is in place." **Degenerate face:** $\rho(x, g) = g$ freezes every regime, and the arena is the disjoint union of the fixed-law worlds. **Persistence is forward-closure:** if $M$ is $\rho$-absorbing ($\rho(x, g) \in M$ for all $x \in X$, $g \in M$), then $[M]$ is forward-closed (1.9) — "installed stays installed" is 1.10's persistence, now for laws.

**J.2 Theorem (21.1 relativized; H.1 relocated).** *(a) The frozen face is the fixed-law reading:* every mechanism event meets each slice $X \times \{g\}$ in $\bot$ or the whole slice — within a fixed-law world, law-level facts are not events; 21.1 is exact, recovered as the degenerate case. *(b) Frozen regimes obey H.1:* if $g$ is $\rho$-absorbing at every state ($\rho(\cdot, g) = g$), then $\iota_g(A) := A \times \{g\}$ is a join-preserving, on-the-nose equivariant embedding ($\hat{f} \circ \iota_g = \iota_g \circ f_g$), so H.1 applies verbatim: no event meeting another regime is ever below the future of a slice event — **a frozen law cannot produce its own reform** (directly: absorbing slices are forward-closed). *(c) Live transitions produce mechanism events:* if $\rho(x, g) \in M$ with $g \notin M$, the atom $(x, g)$ produces $[M]$ at latency $1$; and $[M]$ is producible from a declared origin iff some state along its run carries a transition into $M$. *Proof.* (a) $[M] \cap (X \times \{g\})$ is $\varnothing$ or the slice, by construction. (b) $\hat{f}(\iota_g A) = \{(g(x), \rho(x, g)) : x \in A\} = g[A] \times \{g\} = \iota_g(f_g A)$; any event meeting another regime is new in H.1's sense ($\not\leq \iota_g(C)$ for every $C$). (c) $\hat{f}(x, g) = (g(x), \rho(x, g)) \in X \times M$, so $\hat{f}(\{(x,g)\}) \wedge [M] \neq \bot$ (1.16). $\square$ So H.1 — the theorem that closed G–H to mechanism claims — becomes the **boundary theorem of the regime arena**: it no longer forbids causing mechanisms; it says exactly where that is possible. The escape is H.7's, reused: the law on the larger carrier is *declared anew*, never extended on the nose.

**J.3 Theorem (mechanism claims are exact).** $(\hat{L}, \hat{f})$ is a causal structure, so G.1–G.11 and H.2–H.9 apply to it verbatim; take atomic names among the cylinders. In particular H.10's boundary sentence is now an ordinary expression: $X \mathbin{⤳} [M]$ has realization $\hat{f}^{+}(\langle X \rangle) \wedge [M]$ — "in-force $M$-regimes downstream of an $X$-occurrence" — and exact verdicts (root and strict, H.4; state or token per H.10's decision rule, with the initial event declared, H.7). Mechanisms may cause and be caused at every nesting depth with no new machinery: $[M_1] \mathbin{⤳} [M_2]$ — laws producing laws; $X \mathbin{⤳} ([M] \mathbin{⤳} Y)$ — "$X$ brings it about that the mechanism's presence produces $Y$"; and records of realized mechanism claims persist (H.9): "the mechanism has been installed" is a record event. $\square$

**J.4 Worked arena (the installed sprinkler).** $X = \{p, d, w\}$ (press, dry, wet); $\mathcal{R} = \{g_0, g_1\}$ with $g_0$ (no sprinkler): $p \mapsto d$, $d \mapsto d$, $w \mapsto w$, and $g_1$ (sprinkler): $p \mapsto d$, $d \mapsto w$, $w \mapsto w$; transition: $\rho(p, g_0) = g_1$ — pressing installs — else $\rho(x, g) = g$. Note $M := \{g_1\} = M_{\{d\} \to \{w\}}$ — "dryness gets watered," a mechanism property — and $g_1$ is absorbing. Events: $P := \langle \{p\} \rangle$, $W := \langle \{w\} \rangle$, $[M]$. Declare $I = \{(p, g_0)\}$; the run is $(p, g_0) \to (d, g_1) \to (w, g_1) \to \cdots$. *(i)* $P \mathbin{⤳} [M]$: the realization within reach is $\{(d, g_1), (w, g_1)\} \neq \bot$, and every $[M]$-arrival has the press strictly in its past — $\mathrm{holds}_{I}$ (H.7), with $\tau(P, [M]) = 1$. *(ii)* Nested: $\llbracket [M] \mathbin{⤳} W \rrbracket$ within reach is $\{(w, g_1)\}$ — the wetness the installed mechanism brings about — and $\mathrm{holds}_{I}\big(P \mathbin{⤳} ([M] \mathbin{⤳} W)\big)$: *pressing brought it about that the sprinkler's presence waters the plant.* Note $\tau(P, [M]) = 1 < 2 = \tau(P, W)$: the mechanism is caused before its effects. *(iii)* Contrast $I' = \{(d, g_1)\}$ — pre-installed, nobody pressed: $W$ still occurs and $[M]$ is in force, but $P$ never occurs, so $P \mathbin{⤳} [M]$ is not even possible ($\llbracket \cdot \rrbracket_{I'} = \bot$): installation-caused wetness is discriminated from pre-installed wetness by the same verdicts. *(iv)* After installation, J.2(b) bites: $g_1$ is absorbing, so nothing downstream can cause a return to $g_0$ — un-installation would need a live transition this $\rho$ lacks.

**J.5 Remark (the laboratory internalized; the tower; honesty).** *(a)* Ch. 9 changes mechanisms from outside: an intervention is an exogenous transition, produced by no state — 9.1's separation of frame from theory, surviving here as the difference between $\rho$-moves triggered by world states (production, J.2c) and repertoire moves triggered by the experimenter (surgery). A frame $(f, \mathcal{G})$ with a protocol generates a regime arena — $\mathcal{R}$ the orbit of the law under the repertoire, $\rho$ the protocol's schedule: the laboratory, internalized; a dynamic treatment regime (9.1, 15.2) is exactly an endogenized $\rho$. *(b)* What J does not buy: $\rho$ itself is fixed. "What causes the transition rule?" restarts the question one level up, on carriers from the next floor of the tower (*The Algebra of Laws* §8 — $\mathrm{Law}(\mathrm{Law}(L))$, never closing); each floor opens by declaration only (1.22). The regime arena does not abolish the fixed point of declaration; it relocates it — the stance applied to its own escape hatch. *(c)* Structural honesty: the projection $\pi_X(x, g) = x$ intertwines a fixed world-law only where all in-force regimes agree pointwise — in general the world coordinate obeys no autonomous law. That, precisely, is what "the law is part of the state" says (contrast 1.18iii, where the shadow exists because the law does not vary).
