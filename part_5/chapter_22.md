## Chapter 22. Production, Absence, and Contrast

The case for the production stance, with the book's exhibits. **Absences cause nothing:** "the absence of rain caused the drought" is bookkeeping — the drought was produced by actual processes; absence-talk is *contrast between worlds*, not production within one; in the formalism, $f(\bot) = \bot$ is the stance as an equation (1.6a), and the Kozen boundary (7.1) is its algebraic vindication: refusing absences keeps causation completely axiomatizable. **Production within worlds vs. contrast between worlds:** counterfactuals are comparisons of runs at the same unit (10.1) — no backward causation, no spooky dependence; the arrow of time is the failed complement equations (2.8). **Ontic vs. epistemic:** the schedule exists (determination); data gates access (identification) — 14.3's no-go is the theorem-form. **Token vs. type:** 4.4 and 1.17 — self-causation in reality is type-recurrence walked strictly forward.
