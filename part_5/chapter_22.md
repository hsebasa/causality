## Chapter 22. Determination versus Production

**22.1 Proposition (law-level facts are not events).** Properties of $f$ ("$f$ is injective") hold at no state rather than some — they induce only the trivial events $\bot$ or $\top$: a property of the law is not an occurrence in the world the law governs. $\square$ $f$ *determines* its properties; it does not *produce* them — no occurrence, no time, no latency. (Fixed-law scope: in a regime arena, where the law in force varies with the state, properties of the *current* law are genuine events — Ch. 10 relativizes this proposition without repealing it.)

**22.2 Worked arena ($x^2 \bmod 11$).** $X = \mathbb{Z}/11$, $f(x) = x^2$. Fixed points $\{0, 1\}$; the 4-cycle $(3, 9, 4, 5)$ ($3^2 = 9$, $9^2 = 4$, $4^2 = 5$, $5^2 = 3$); Garden-of-Eden states $=$ the non-residues $\{2, 6, 7, 8, 10\}$ (never produced); merging from clause interaction ($x$ and $-x$ collide) — the squaring witness of 2.8 lives here: $f[\{2\} \wedge \{9\}] = \bot$ but $f[\{2\}] \wedge f[\{9\}] = \{4\}$. One small arena exhibiting recurrence skeleton, transient anatomy, uncaused states, and merging at once (5.3 in miniature).

**22.3 Witness asymmetry.** A *proof* is a **fossil**: a dependency record witnessing determination — not a producer. Determination is witnessed by a proof object, timelessly; production is witnessed by an occurrence in a run, datedly. Conflating them is the implication-vs-causation error at the meta-level — the error the program was founded on refusing. The refusal now has an exact measure: one fossil supports many productions — presentations vary depth and route, declarations vary direction (21.3–21.4).
