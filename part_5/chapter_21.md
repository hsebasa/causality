## Chapter 21. The Proving Arena

**21.1 Definition (the dictionary).** States $=$ deductively closed theories; law $=$ one-step saturation (apply every inference rule once); events $=$ positive assertions ("$\varphi$ is established"); **latency $=$ proof depth; unprovability $=$ absence** — the production stance doing real work: what is never derived simply never occurs. **Proposition:** the saturation law is inflationary ($\Gamma \subseteq f(\Gamma)$), so reachability is antisymmetric — AC is a *theorem* here (4.2b). **Proposition (continuity $=$ finitariness):** the law is join-preserving iff the inference rules are finitary; the $\omega$-rule is the discontinuous law — Example 1.15 in proof-theoretic clothing.

**21.2 Labels and interventions.** Labelled (ATMS-style) arenas track *sets of support*: a theorem with several derivations carries several labels — overdetermination of theorems handled at the token level. Axiom-removal interventions have **one-signed effects**: deleting an axiom never adds theorems (monotone deletion) — a frame whose every surgery moves the world in one direction. In the algebra of laws: a rule set enters as a join of one-rule laws, surgery moves along the pointwise order, and one-signedness is monotonicity of the star (*The Algebra of Laws* §5).

**21.3 Theorem (presentation relativity: one entailment, many calculi).** A **presentation** is a rule set $R$; its law is a join of one-rule laws, $f_R = \mathrm{id} \vee \bigvee_{r \in R} f_r$ (*The Algebra of Laws* §1; the unit is 21.1's inflationarity). In the single-premise fragment the dictionary sharpens: statements themselves are states, each rule an edge (2.3), derivability the star, proof depth the latency (1.16). *(a) The causal order is a closure-fact:* presentations with equal star have identical derivability, and on statements $\sqsubseteq$ (1.11′) *is* the derivability preorder ($\psi \in f^{\ast}\{\varphi\} \iff \varphi \vdash \psi$) — invariant across every presentation of one entailment space. *(b) Proof depth is not:* $R_1 = \{\varphi \to \psi,\ \psi \to \chi\}$ and $R_2 = R_1 \cup \{\varphi \to \chi\}$ — the derived rule promoted to primitive — have equal stars while $\tau(\{\varphi\}, \{\chi\})$ drops from $2$ to $1$; parents and the direct-vs-mediated split move with it. *(c) The space of presentations:* mutually derivable statements are cycles, and the condensation (4.1) is the Lindenbaum order; under AC the presentations of a fixed derivability order form the fiber of the star — 6.4a's interval $[\mathrm{cov}(d), d^{\circ}]$ in the lattice of laws, the free Boolean cube on the mediated entailments — so a theory with infinitely many mediated entailments has uncountably many presentations; in general 6.4(i)'s bookkeeping (law $=$ space $+$ generator) holds with no hypotheses. *Proof.* (a) $\sqsubseteq$ and derivability are $f^{\ast}$-facts. (b) $f_{R_1}\{\varphi\} = \{\psi\}$, $f_{R_1}^{2}\{\varphi\} = \{\chi\}$, while $f_{R_2}\{\varphi\} = \{\psi, \chi\}$; the stars agree at every state. (c) is 6.4a and 6.4(i). $\square$ **The dictionary of fiber motion.** Up $=$ promoting a derived rule (a mediated link asserted primitive); down $=$ lemma-inlining, elimination of a detour (cut-style); bottom $\mathrm{cov}(d)$ $=$ the minimal calculus; top $d^{\circ}$ $=$ every admissible shortcut primitive. Fiber-mates are separated by surgery and by tokens, never by theorems: delete the lemma $\psi$ and $\varphi \rightsquigarrow \chi$ dies under $R_1$ and survives under $R_2$ — 6.4's witness *is* lemma-inlining — and their routes differ (8.8b), which is what 21.2's labels record. Same theorems, different mechanism: 11.3's autonomy, in mathematics. **Multi-premise rules** make the one-step relation on statements a hypergraph; 21.1's theories-as-states absorb them (a rule fires on any theory containing its premises), the law stays a join of one-rule laws, and the presentation-invariant becomes the induced closure $\Gamma \mapsto \bigvee_n f^{n}(\Gamma)$ — depths and routes presentation-relative exactly as above. **Reading.** Proof depth, parenthood, directness are properties of the presentation; what all presentations share is the star. *Logic is the space; the calculus is the generator; production lives in the generator.*

**21.4 The contraposed fossil: direction is declared.** One law, both orientations. States: the subsets of $\Phi = \{A,\ B,\ \neg A,\ \neg B,\ A \Rightarrow B\}$; rules MP ($\{A,\ A \Rightarrow B\} \vdash B$) and MT ($\{\neg B,\ A \Rightarrow B\} \vdash \neg A$); law $f = \mathrm{id} \vee f_{\mathrm{MP}} \vee f_{\mathrm{MT}}$ — sharp, inflationary (21.1). Declare $I_1 = \{A,\ A \Rightarrow B\}$: $f(I_1) \ni B$ — the establishment of $A$ produces the establishment of $B$ at latency $1$. Declare $I_2 = \{\neg B,\ A \Rightarrow B\}$: $f(I_2) \ni \neg A$ — $\neg B$ produces $\neg A$, latency $1$. **Same law, same fossil, opposite productions across one implication: the direction is fixed by the declared initial event alone** (8.7: $I$ is load-bearing) — no change of calculus needed, hence no subjectivity either: the goal ("establish $B$"; "refute $A$") selects the declaration, and given the declaration the causal facts are exact. Facts relative to declared structure (1.22); one world, many laboratories (12.1) — **the calculus is the laboratory of mathematics**. Hygiene: the events are establishments — "$\neg B$ is established" is a positive occurrence, not an absence; 21.1's "unprovability $=$ absence" and the stance of Ch. 23 are untouched. **The classical boundary.** In any calculus deriving contraposition, the axiom itself may be stored as $A \Rightarrow B$ or as $\neg B \Rightarrow \neg A$ with the same closure: contraposition is an involution on presentations that the closure cannot see (the toy, having no contraposition rule, shows the sharper fact: the flip needs no such symmetry — $I$ suffices). Constructively the involution fails: $A \Rightarrow B$ yields $\neg B \Rightarrow \neg A$, but the converse returns only $A \Rightarrow \neg\neg B$ — double-negation elimination is exactly the classical step — so intuitionistic arenas keep orientation in the closure itself: direction becomes content, and the fiber shrinks. The eraser is the complement, as everywhere in the book — both complement laws are reversibility (2.8), complement crosses out of complete axiomatizability (G.1) — and classical negation is the object-language complement: it is what renders orientation closure-invisible; the production stance restores the arrow by declaration, not by metaphysics. **Queries, well-posed.** Once $(R, I)$ is declared: "what does $A$ cause?" is the cone $f^{+}(E_A)$ (7.1) within reach of $I$; "what causes $B$?" is abduction $f^{\sharp}(E_B)$ (2.1) at type level, routes and supports at token level (8.7–8.9; 21.2's labels). 21.5 varies the initial event systematically — reverse mathematics: which $I$ reach $T$ — as proof analysis varies the generator (21.3): the two axes, worked as disciplines. **Coda.** Mathematics is not causally empty; it is causally *underdetermined until presented*. Determination is what all presentations share; production is what a presentation adds; reading direction into the shared part is the implication-vs-causation error (22.3), and reading one calculus as canonical is its mirror image. The cure is the book's oldest commitment: declare.

**21.5 Identification as reverse mathematics.** Reverse mathematics *is* identification theory: which axioms (frames) make which theorems (effects) recoverable — "what must be assumed for this to be derivable" and "what must be observed/intervened for this to be identified" are one question; in the terms of 21.3–21.4, reverse mathematics varies the declared initial event, as proof analysis varies the generator. The completeness landscape of App. G, revisited from inside: the proving arena's equational algebra is the complete positive fragment; its first-order theory the incomplete one. The decidability separation (G.3) reads: causation is simple; experimentation is where undecidability enters. The identification arena of Ch. 23 makes this algorithmic: identified $=$ reachable, hedges $=$ Garden-of-Eden.
