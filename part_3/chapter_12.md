## Chapter 12. Frames: The Laboratory

**12.1 Definition (interventions; frames; protocols).** An *intervention* is designated exogenous structure: a state map $g : X \to X$ or a mechanism surgery $f_v \mapsto f_v'$. An **experimental frame** equips a theory with a repertoire $\mathcal{G}$; a **protocol** interleaves the law with repertoire elements, including history-dependent assignments — static, dynamic, and natural-value regimes uniformly (static protocols are words in the quantale of laws, *The Algebra of Laws* §4). Frames are separate from the theory — one world, many laboratories: *interventions are the price of mathematics and experiment, never of causation*. A splitting induces the **canonical assignment repertoire** (replace $f_W$ by a constant). **Noise typing:** $\delta$ (the implementer's randomizer — frame-side, independent by construction) vs. $\epsilon$ (the world's — whatever the model says).

**12.2 Surgeries; derived SWIGs.** *Incoming* surgery ($f_W := w$) destroys the natural value. **Outgoing surgery (node-splitting):** keep $f_W$; precompose every *other* mechanism with "read $w$ in the $W$-slot." **Proposition (the SWIG is a derived graph).** By 11.2 applied to the surgered law: $W$ keeps its incoming edges (its mechanism is untouched) and loses its outgoing ones (rerouted mechanisms are constant in $x_W$) — the node-split picture, derived; the derived graph at a *specific* $w$ can be strictly sparser than the union-over-$w$ template (context-specific independence). $\square$ **Notation:** label random nodes $V(a_V)$ with *ancestral* fixed nodes only (minimal labels — exclusion restrictions automatic); regard $\mathcal{G}(\cdot)$ as a *template*, a graph-valued function on the value domain.
