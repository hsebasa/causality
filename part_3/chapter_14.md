## Chapter 14. Inference Before Probability

**14.1 Proposition (revealability — the fundamental problem, exact).** The natural run at unit $u$ determines the schedule exactly on the set $\{\iota : \iota \text{ acts as the identity along the realized trajectory}\}$ — by abstract consistency those runs coincide with the natural one — and beyond it determines nothing except membership constraints. $\square$ One world per unit is *witnessed*; the rest are absences: a measure-free $\Sigma/\Pi$ statement.

**14.2 Response types; possibilistic identifiability.** The schedule induces $\rho : U \to \prod_\iota \mathrm{Val}$; its fibers (response types) partition the units with no measure. Monotonicity ("no defiers") is *emptiness of a cell*. On $\{W(u) = w\}$, $Y(w)(u)$ is revealed exactly; off it, confined to its cell's value set. **Possibilistic identifiability:** $\Psi$ is identified iff constant across all models compatible with every unit's revealed data — set-valued deterministic bounds, of which Manski-style partial identification is the measured shadow.

**14.3 Theorem (exact tests from the frame's coin alone).** Under the sharp null ($Y(\iota)(u) = Y(u)$ for all $u$ and all $\iota$ in the design), the observed outcome panel is invariant under reassignment, so any test statistic's distribution under the frame randomizer $\delta$ — lab-side, known by construction — is exactly computable: Fisher-style randomization inference with **zero** world-side measure. $\square$ The $\delta/\epsilon$ typing pays its way: exact tests live entirely in the laboratory.

**14.4 Monotone comparative statics.** Monotone mechanisms compose to monotone potential-outcome maps — pure order theory, available before any measure.
