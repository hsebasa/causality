## Chapter 12. Morphisms, Transfer, Realization

**12.1 Theorem (Pullback Transfer).** A *simulation* $h : \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{H}$: $h \circ f_E = f_H \circ h$; event pullback $h^{-1}(\mathcal{O}_H) \subseteq \mathcal{O}_E$; matched repertoires ($h \circ g = g' \circ h$). *For any simulation, events $A', B'$, state, matched protocol: $A' \rightsquigarrow B'$ in $\mathcal{H}$ from $h(x_0)$ iff $h^{-1}A' \rightsquigarrow h^{-1}B'$ in $\mathcal{E}$ from $x_0$ — with latencies exactly equal.* *Proof:* equivariance pointwise in time: $f_H^{\,t}(h x_0) = h(f_E^{\,t} x_0)$. $\square$ **Three-tier grammar:** pull back always; push forward along quotients for saturated events; isomorphism for total two-way transfer. Abstract interpretation, bisimulation, "use linear algebra on a program" are tiers 1–2 and licensed.

**12.2\* Categorical dividends.** The adjoint triple $\mathrm{Basins} \dashv \Delta \dashv \mathrm{Fix}$ (destiny classes and equilibria as the two adjoints of forgetting dynamics); **minimal realization** — the causal content of a model with respect to an observation structure; a qualitative query is identified iff it factors through the minimal realization (the Nerode-style algorithm of Ch. 23).
