## Chapter 8. Nested Causal Claims: Realization and Verdict

*Chapter 7 internalized the carrier of causation — the connective $\triangleright$, $A \triangleright B = f^{+}(A) \wedge B$ — but left "causes" itself as a judgment ($A \triangleright B = B$, 7.2c), and the carrier alone cannot bear nesting: $A \triangleright (B \triangleright C) = f^{+}(A) \wedge f^{+}(B) \wedge C = B \triangleright (A \triangleright C)$ (7.4e). One might hope to repair this by enlarging the lattice — making "$A$ causes $B$" a new element and extending the law to it. 8.1 proves that road closed: over a fixed law the truth of a causal claim is an eternal fact, and an eternal fact can neither produce nor be produced. The repair is a division of labor. Every causal expression carries **two** components, both already in the theory: its **realization** — the event "this pattern is occurring", 7.3's denotation, an element of $L$ — and its **verdict** — the exact judgment of 7.2(c), issued at every nesting level. Slots take realizations (only occurrences can produce); levels take verdicts. Nothing is approximated: the test at every node is full strict production of realizations, $\llbracket t \rrbracket \leq f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket)$. 8.7–8.9 run the same recursion in the history structure (1.18), where "every occurrence" means every arrival-with-route; there realized causal facts persist and can themselves be caused. 8.10 says which home answers which question.*

**8.1 Proposition (no dynamics for new elements — why not enlarge the lattice).** *Let $\iota : L \to L'$ be a join-preserving embedding into a causal structure $(L', f')$ with $f' \circ \iota = \iota \circ f$ (the law extended on the nose), and let $\tau \in L'$ be new: $\tau \not\leq \iota(c)$ for every $c \in L$. Then $\tau \not\leq f'^{\ast}(\iota(X))$ for every $X \in L$ — under the causal preorder of $(L', f')$ (1.11′), no ordinary event ever causes a new element.* *Proof.* $f'^{n}(\iota X) = \iota(f^{n} X)$ by induction on $n$; hence $f'^{\ast}(\iota X) = \bigvee_{n} f'^{n}(\iota X) = \iota\big(\bigvee_{n} f^{n} X\big) = \iota(f^{\ast} X)$ (1.14 in $(L', f')$, then join-preservation of $\iota$); and $\tau \not\leq \iota(f^{\ast} X)$ by newness. $\square$ So a claim promoted to a new lattice element is unreachable from the world it is about; and its truth value — fixed once and for all by $(L, f)$ — offers the law nothing to act on. The two jobs a nested claim must do, *occupying an event-slot* and *being true*, require two different objects.

**8.2 Definition (expressions; realization; verdict).** Fix $(L, f)$ and atomic names $\mathrm{At} \subseteq L$ (7.3). **Expressions:** $t ::= a \mid t \vee t \mid t \wedge t \mid t \mathbin{⤳} t$ ($a \in \mathrm{At}$); read $s \mathbin{⤳} t$ as "$s$ causes $t$" — the former is written $⤳$ to keep the judged claim distinct from its carrier $\triangleright$. The **realization** $\llbracket\,\cdot\,\rrbracket : \mathrm{Expr} \to L$ is 7.3's denotation under $⤳ \mapsto \triangleright$:
$$\llbracket a \rrbracket = a,\quad \llbracket s \vee t \rrbracket = \llbracket s \rrbracket \vee \llbracket t \rrbracket,\quad \llbracket s \wedge t \rrbracket = \llbracket s \rrbracket \wedge \llbracket t \rrbracket,\quad \llbracket s \mathbin{⤳} t \rrbracket = f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket) \wedge \llbracket t \rrbracket.$$
The **verdict** of each $⤳$-node is the exact claim, judged on realizations:
$$\mathrm{holds}(s \mathbin{⤳} t) \;:\iff\; \bot \neq \llbracket t \rrbracket \leq f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket) \;\iff\; \llbracket s \mathbin{⤳} t \rrbracket = \llbracket t \rrbracket \neq \bot$$
— full strict production (7.2c), with the guard $\neq \bot$ barring vacuous truths (a holding claim's realization is a possible event, 7.3 *Queries*): "everything causes the impossible" is quarantined as the diagonal was by $n \geq 1$ (1.16). The **verdict of an expression** is the verdict at its root; inner verdicts remain available (8.4). Everything is defined for every expression over every causal structure — no atoms, no frame, no new lattice, no extension of $f$.

**8.3 Remark (division of labor).** *(a) Slots take realizations.* A verdict is a truth value and produces nothing; what acts in a cause- or conclusion-slot is the pattern's occurrences. In "the rain's causing the flood caused the evacuation", the subject is the flood-as-produced-by-rain — an event — not the truth value of "rain causes flood". *(b) The law acts through realizations.* $f$ applies to every expression via $\llbracket\,\cdot\,\rrbracket$, and
$$f\big(\llbracket s \mathbin{⤳} t \rrbracket\big) \;\leq\; f^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket) \wedge f(\llbracket t \rrbracket) \;=\; \llbracket s \rrbracket \triangleright f(\llbracket t \rrbracket)$$
(1.6c, and $f(f^{+}A) = \bigvee_{n \geq 2} f^{n}A \leq f^{+}A$): advancing a realized claim advances its conclusion and holds its cause — a theorem, not a stipulation. Verdicts do not evolve: over a fixed law they are eternal (8.1); persistence of *realized* facts lives at the token level (8.9). *(c) The logic of claims is Boolean, not lattice-theoretic.* The conjunction of the claims "$A$ causes $B$" and "$A'$ causes $B'$" is a conjunction of verdicts — ordinary logic, outside $L$. The meet $\llbracket s \mathbin{⤳} t \rrbracket \wedge \llbracket s' \mathbin{⤳} t' \rrbracket$ is a different, useful event: co-occurrence of the realized instances. Each lives where it belongs; conflating them is what an enlarged lattice would force.

**8.4 Definition (root vs. strict verdict).** The **root verdict** of $E$ is $\mathrm{holds}$ at its outermost $⤳$-node: outer claims quantify over *realized* inner instances and presuppose no inner universal ("the rain's causing the flood caused the panic" does not assert that every flood is rain-caused). The **strict verdict** $\mathrm{holds}^{!}(E)$ is the conjunction of $\mathrm{holds}$ over every $⤳$-node of $E$. Both are exact; an application must declare which it asks. Default in this book: the root verdict.

**8.5 Proposition (nesting is faithful at the verdict level).** *Right-nested twins share a realization but not a verdict:*
$$\llbracket A \mathbin{⤳} (B \mathbin{⤳} C) \rrbracket \;=\; f^{+}(A) \wedge f^{+}(B) \wedge C \;=\; \llbracket B \mathbin{⤳} (A \mathbin{⤳} C) \rrbracket \qquad (7.4e),$$
*while $\mathrm{holds}(A \mathbin{⤳} (B \mathbin{⤳} C)) \iff \bot \neq f^{+}(B) \wedge C \leq f^{+}(A)$ and $\mathrm{holds}(B \mathbin{⤳} (A \mathbin{⤳} C)) \iff \bot \neq f^{+}(A) \wedge C \leq f^{+}(B)$ — inequivalent.* *Witness.* $X = \{a, b, d, c, e\}$, $R : a \mapsto \{b, d\},\ b \mapsto c,\ d \mapsto e$, $c, e$ fixed; $A = \{a\}$, $B = \{b\}$, $C = \{c, e\}$. Then $f^{+}(B) \wedge C = \{c\} \leq f^{+}(A) = \{b, d, c, e\}$: the first holds — every $B$-produced $C$-occurrence is $A$-producible. But $f^{+}(A) \wedge C = \{c, e\} \not\leq f^{+}(B) = \{c\}$: the second fails — $e$ is $A$-producible and never $B$-producible. Same realization $\{c\}$, opposite verdicts. $\square$ The exchange symmetry of $\triangleright$ (7.4e) is a fact about realizations only; the claims are separated by their verdicts. (Under the strict verdict the first fails too, via its inner node $B \mathbin{⤳} C$ — the two verdicts answer different questions, 8.4.)

**8.6 Worked evaluation (the general expression of 7.6i).** $E = (A \mathbin{⤳} X) \mathbin{⤳} \big(B \mathbin{⤳} (C \mathbin{⤳} (D \vee (H \mathbin{⤳} F)))\big)$. Inside-out, realizations $R_{i}$ with a verdict at each $⤳$-node:
$$R_{0} = f^{+}(A) \wedge X,\ \ v_{0}: \bot \neq X \leq f^{+}(A); \qquad R_{1} = f^{+}(H) \wedge F,\ \ v_{1}: \bot \neq F \leq f^{+}(H); \qquad R_{2} = D \vee R_{1}\ \text{(join node, no verdict)};$$
$$R_{3} = f^{+}(C) \wedge R_{2},\ \ v_{2}: \bot \neq R_{2} \leq f^{+}(C); \qquad R_{4} = f^{+}(B) \wedge R_{3},\ \ v_{3}: \bot \neq R_{3} \leq f^{+}(B);$$
$$\llbracket E \rrbracket = f^{+}(R_{0}) \wedge R_{4} \ \text{— the element of 7.6(i)} \qquad v: \ \bot \neq f^{+}(B) \wedge f^{+}(C) \wedge \big(D \vee (f^{+}(H) \wedge F)\big) \leq f^{+}\big(f^{+}(A) \wedge X\big).$$
The root verdict $v$ reads: *every occurrence of "$D$, or the $H$-produced $F$, downstream of both $B$ and $C$" lies strictly downstream of an $X$-occurrence that $A$ produced.* **Instance.** States $\{0, \dots, 6\}$, $f(i) = \min(i{+}1, 6)$; $A = \{0\}$, $X = \{1\}$, $B = \{2\}$, $C = \{3\}$, $H = \{4\}$, $F = \{5\}$, $D = \{0, 5\}$. Then $v_{0}$ holds ($\{1\} \leq \{1,\dots,6\}$; $R_{0} = \{1\}$); $v_{1}$ holds ($\{5\} \leq \{5,6\}$; $R_{1} = \{5\}$); $R_{2} = \{0,5\}$; $v_{2}$ **fails** ($0 \notin f^{+}\{3\} = \{4,5,6\}$), and $R_{3} = \{5\}$ — the realization discards the $D$-occurrence at $0$ before the outer layers see it; $v_{3}$ holds ($\{5\} \leq \{3,\dots,6\}$; $R_{4} = \{5\}$); the root holds ($\{5\} \leq f^{+}\{1\} = \{2,\dots,6\}$), with $\llbracket E \rrbracket = \{5\}$ and $f(\llbracket E \rrbracket) = \{6\}$. Root verdict true, strict verdict false via $v_{2}$: both exact, both reported.

**8.7 Definition (token semantics: the same recursion on histories).** The verdicts of 8.2 read "every occurrence of $B$" as every $B$-*state*: they assert producibility — $B$ inside a cone — and are blind to *routes*. For the stronger reading — every arrival of $B$ actually produced by the cause — run the same recursion in the history structure (1.18), in the canonical presentation $(X, R)$ (1.4, 1.7a), relativized to a declared **initial event** $I$ (facts relative to declared structure, 1.22): states the finite runs $h = (x_{0}, \dots, x_{t})$ with $x_{0} \in I$ and each $x_{i+1} \in R(x_{i})$; law $F$ = append one step (a causal structure, 1.18i–ii); **lift** $\langle A \rangle := \{h : x_{t} \in A\}$, "$A$ occurs now". The lift preserves arbitrary joins and meets but is **lax** for the law: $F\langle A \rangle \leq \langle f(A) \rangle$, strict whenever an $f(A)$-state has another route in or lies in $I$ — $F\langle A \rangle$ is "an $f(A)$-occurrence that $A$ *just produced*", and the deficit is provenance. This laxity is the escape from 8.1: no on-the-nose $\iota$ exists here, and type-level facts return only as shadows along $\pi$ (1.18iii). **Token realization and verdict:** 8.2's recursion in $(\mathcal{P}(\mathrm{Hist}), F)$ over lifted atoms — $\llbracket a \rrbracket_{I} = \langle a \rangle$, $\llbracket s \mathbin{⤳} t \rrbracket_{I} = F^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket_{I}) \wedge \llbracket t \rrbracket_{I}$, and $\mathrm{holds}_{I}(s \mathbin{⤳} t) :\iff \bot \neq \llbracket t \rrbracket_{I} \leq F^{+}(\llbracket s \rrbracket_{I})$. Since the token structure is a causal structure, 7.1–7.11 and 8.2–8.5 apply to it verbatim; only the reading changes: an occurrence is an **arrival** — a state together with the route that produced it — and $F^{+}(P)$ is "$P$ lies strictly in the past". $I$ is load-bearing: with $I = \top$ every state is also a fresh, past-free arrival, and every nonzero token universal fails — the token reading forces genuine origins to be declared. For sharp laws with a one-state $I$, the unrolling is an equivalent lightweight home (1.18iv).

**8.8 Proposition (bridge; and where the two homes part).** *(a) Token implies state, on the reachable part:* if $\mathrm{holds}_{I}(A \mathbin{⤳} B)$ for base $A, B$, then $\bot \neq B \wedge f^{\ast}(I) \leq f^{+}(A)$; if moreover $B \leq f^{\ast}(I)$, then $\mathrm{holds}(A \mathbin{⤳} B)$. *Proof.* A history ending at a $B$-state $b$ exists iff $b \in f^{\ast}(I)$; it lies in $\langle B \rangle \leq F^{+}\langle A \rangle$, so some proper prefix ends in $A$, whence $b \in f^{+}(A)$. $\square$ The converse fails in exactly two ways. *(b) Merges.* $X = \{a, z, c\}$: $a \to c \leftarrow z$, $c$ fixed, $I = \{a, z\}$. State level: $\{c\} \leq f^{+}\{a\}$ — "$A$ causes $C$" holds; every $C$-state is *producible from* $A$. Token level: the arrival $(z, c)$ has no $A$ in its past — fails; not every $C$-occurrence is *produced by* $A$. Reachability cannot see who made $c$; routes can. *(c) Origins.* The clock (1.7c), $I = \{8\}$: state level, $\{8\} \leq f^{+}\{9\}$ — "9 o'clock causes 8 o'clock" holds; on a cycle everything is producible from everything. Token level: the arrival $(8)$ is past-free — fails. Yet $\llbracket 8 \mathbin{⤳} 8 \rrbracket_{I} \ni (8, 9, \dots, 8) \neq \bot$: the 24-step return is *realized* without being *universal* — 1.17's tokens, now doing semantic work.

**8.9 Proposition (records; claims as causes and effects).** For a token event $P$ set $\mathrm{rec}(P) := F^{\ast}(P)$ — "$P$ has been realized". *(i) Records persist:* $F(\mathrm{rec}(P)) \leq \mathrm{rec}(P)$, since $F^{\ast}$-values are forward-closed (Lemma 1.11) — realized causal facts, once achieved, stay achieved: 1.10's "the message has been sent", now a theorem about claims. *(ii) Higher-order claims have content:* token realizations and records are ordinary events, so they enter production (1.16) and $\sqsubseteq$ (1.11′) like any event — $\mathrm{holds}_{I}\big(x \mathbin{⤳} (s \mathbin{⤳} t)\big)$ says every realization of $s \mathbin{⤳} t$ descends from an $x$-occurrence, and $\mathrm{holds}_{I}\big((s \mathbin{⤳} t) \mathbin{⤳} y\big)$ says every $y$-arrival descends from a completed realization; neither is vacuous. $\square$ Contrast 8.1: this is possible only because the lift is lax (8.7); an on-the-nose extension provably blocks it.

**8.10 Remark (which home answers which question; boundary).** *Decision rule.* Whether a pattern is **producible** — coverage, cones, type-level claims — is asked and answered at the state level (8.2–8.6): fully abstract, and the verdicts are the book's existing judgments (7.2) made compositional. Who **made** which occurrence — routes, tokens, records, higher-order claims — is asked at the token level (8.7–8.9), with $I$ declared. The state calculus is the $\pi$-shadow of the token calculus (1.18iii); token verdicts are strictly stronger on the reachable part (8.8). Both are exact; they answer different questions, and an application must say which it asks. *Boundary.* Verdicts are eternal: this appendix evaluates causal claims; it does not make their truth contingent. "$X$ brings it about that the *mechanism* $A \to B$ is in place" requires a law that varies with the state — the regime-change reading (7.7c), folding the law into the state — and lies outside the one-fixed-law scope of this appendix (the natural carrier for such states, the lattice of mechanisms itself, is assembled in *The Algebra of Laws* §8; the home is built in Ch. 10).

**8.11 Remark (the homes of realization).** The recursion of 8.2 consumes an event structure, a law, and a lift of the atoms — nothing else — so it runs verbatim in any causal structure; call the pair (structure, lift) a **home**. 8.7 was the first re-homing; realizations transport laxly along morphisms of $\mathbf{Caus}$ (7.7a), and lax lifts strengthen verdicts (8.8). The map: **routes** — the token home (8.7–8.9): who made which occurrence. **Degree, strength, depth** — the graded home over a quantale (Ch. 9): the verdict itself becomes $V$-valued. **Mechanism change** — the regime arena (Ch. 10), whose states carry the law in force (*The Algebra of Laws* §8): law-level facts become events, lawfully. **Chance** — deliberately *not* a home: the stochastic face has no idempotent join and no convergent star (5.2, 2.5iii), so there is no measure-valued $\llbracket\,\cdot\,\rrbracket$; the measure enters once and late (1.22, Ch. 16, App. B), by evaluating realizations — $\mu(\llbracket E \rrbracket_{I})$ on the token home is the probability that the pattern is realized, while contrasts of verdicts across schedules are the gated cross-world quantities of 17.3. **Absence and contrast** — refused as homes by the stance (Ch. 23): contrast lives between runs (13.1), not inside a realization. One grammar; several homes; each answering exactly the question the fixed-law state calculus provably cannot (8.1, 8.10, 22.1, 17.3).
