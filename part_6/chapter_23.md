## Chapter 23. The Production Stance and the Program

**23.1 Production, absence, and contrast.** The case for the production stance, with the book's exhibits. **Absences cause nothing:** "the absence of rain caused the drought" is bookkeeping — the drought was produced by actual processes; absence-talk is *contrast between worlds*, not production within one; in the formalism, $f(\bot) = \bot$ is the stance as an equation (1.6a), and the Kozen boundary (G.1) is its algebraic vindication: refusing absences keeps causation completely axiomatizable. **Production within worlds vs. contrast between worlds:** counterfactuals are comparisons of runs at the same unit (13.1) — no backward causation, no spooky dependence; the arrow of time is the failed complement equations (2.8). **Ontic vs. epistemic:** the schedule exists (determination); data gates access (identification) — 17.3's no-go is the theorem-form. **Token vs. type:** 4.4 and 1.17 — self-causation in reality is type-recurrence walked strictly forward.

**23.2 Open problems and the program.** Bi-interpretability (one-intervener vs. category theory); intrinsic factorizability; the full iff for star-reflection invertibility at arbitrary $V$; transmission criterion; pushout transport; classification programs beyond finite arenas; binary proxy worked instance; two-unit interference example; **Lean mechanization** — the first machine-checked foundation of potential outcomes. **The general identification algorithm.** At the split, variable-observed, positive face the complete algorithm exists and *imports* (Tian–Pearl; Shpitser–Pearl; Huang–Valtorta; fixing/nested Markov; po-calculus — all ledger-gated). Native reformulation to develop: **identification as reachability in a rewriting arena** — states $=$ expressions in observables, law $=$ the frame-licensed moves (marginalize, condition, fix $=$ outgoing surgery $+$ condition), identified $=$ reachable from the observed corner, hedges $=$ the Garden-of-Eden events of that arena — making identification theory itself a causal theory and 21.5 algorithmic. Open: (i) the *coarse-observation* case — when $\mathcal{O}$ is a genuine sub-lattice rather than a sub-splitting, does a finite combinatorial criterion exist for the structural class? (19.2's class-relativity already shows the class must be an input); (ii) finite measured classes with declared semialgebraic axioms are decidable in principle by real quantifier elimination (algebraic-statistics lineage [verify]) — Pearl's algorithm is the miracle collapse of that decision to polynomial graph syntax, and whether a similar collapse exists for coarse $\mathcal{O}$ is the prize; (iii) the qualitative/possibilistic analog is **minimal realization**: for finite structures, a production query is identified iff invariant on the observational-equivalence class, decided by computing the canonical minimal realization (Nerode-style) — tying 15.2$^{\ast}$'s open item to identification; (iv) full generality conjectured undecidable (C2 inheritance, App. G).
